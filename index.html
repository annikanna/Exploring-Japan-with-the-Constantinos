EXPLORING JAPAN

Annika Kim Constantino

THE EXPLORERS: THE CONSTANTINO FAMILY

For as long as I can remember, my family and I have been avid travelers. From London, Rome, Switzerland, South Korea, to the Philippines, we choose to spend our vacations exploring a new place and indulging in its culture, food, and people. Our most favorite place that we’ve traveled to so far is Japan! We first traveled there in June 2018 after my high school graduation. We enjoyed it so much that we traveled to Japan again a few months ago in 2019, spending Thanksgiving and part of December there! This blog will specifically feature the experiences we had on this most recent trip. It will be broken down by places we’ve been to: Tokyo, Yokohama, Nara, and Hiroshima.

THE TRIP

TOKYO

Tokyo is Japan’s capital and perhaps one of the world’s most well-known and populous cities. We flew in from San Francisco to Narita airport, where we took a train all the way to Shinjuku, one of the 23 city wards of Tokyo. Our Airbnb was there so we spent the first few days exploring notable landmarks in Tokyo.

Akihabara

We spent the first part of our day in Akihabara, or the "electric city"! We browsed around several electronic stores and visisted famous spots like the SEGA store!

Akihabara Info
Food Highlight: Katsu Curry

The best meal we had that day was katsu curry at a restaurant in Akihabara! We each ordered a plate of pork katsu with curry, rice, and radishes. It was the best katsu curry I've ever had!

Katsu Curry Info
Imperial Palace

After Akihabara, we went to Tokyo Imperial Palace, the main residence of the Imperial family. We walked around the palace grounds and explored the palace garden!

Imperial Palace Info
Zojoji Temple

We spent the last part of our day at Zojoji Temple, a Buddhist temple at the bottom of Tokyo Tower! We walked around the temple grounds and entered the main templed building!

Zojoji Temple Info

YOKOKHAMA

Yokohama is at the center of Japan, along the coastline of Japan’s Pacific Ocean. It is a quaint seaside town that we decided to take a day trip to. We took a one hour bullet train and walked by foot to numerous landmarks around Yokohama.

Previous Next
The Yokohama Brick Warehouse

After riding the bullet train, we made our way to the famous Yokohama Brick Warehouse! It is several historic, red brick buildings along Yokohama bay that have been converted into one of the most popular family, date, and shopping hubs of Yokohama. Inside we browsed at a variety of unique shops, and outside, we enjoyed a Christmas marktet with a skating rink.

Brick Warehouse Info
Food Highlight: Omurice

The best meal we had that day was omurice at one of the restaurants in the Yokohama Brick Warehouse! The restaurant had an open grill where you can watch the chefs create the dish - covering ketchup flavored rice with a thin omelet and a salty but sweet sauce. My sister and I shared an omurice with some pork katsu as a topping as well.

Omurice Info
Cupnoodles Museum

After lunch, we made our way to the Cupnoodles museum of Yokohama! We learned about how the inventor, Momofuku Ando, first created cupnoodles. Additionally, we enjoyed a lot of photo ops and an activity that allowed us to creatre our own, custom cupnoodle! My sister and I got to choose our broth, noodles, seasoning, topping, and got to decorate the cup!

Cupnoodles Museum Info
Shin-Yokohama Ramen Museum

We ended the day with dinner at the Shin-Yokohama ramen museum! The top floor acts as a museum, featuring the history of different kinds of ramen, ramen utensils like bowls and chopsticks, as well as an interactive ramen making exhibit. The downstairs floor is a giant food court of mulitple ramen restaurants! We enjoyed fresh gyoza and tonkatsu ramen.

Ramen Museum Info

NARA

After Hiroshima, we took a bullet train to Nara. It is a city in the Nara prefecture located less than one hour from Kyoto and Osaka. Due to its past as the first permanent capital, the city remains full of historic treasures, including some of Japan's oldest and largest temples. We spent most of the day at the Nara Deer park and Todaiji Temple.

Previous Next
Nara Deer Park

We spent most of the day at this large park in central Nara. It is the location of many of Nara's main attractions including Todaiji, Kasuga Taisha, Kofukuji and the Nara National Museum. It is also home to hundreds of freely roaming deer. We enjoyed feeding the deer with deer crackers, strolling around the park and lake, and eating street food!

Nara Deer Park Info
Food Highlight: Mochi

After visiting the deer park we got a snack at a famous mochi shop in the heart of Nara named Nakatanidou. It is not only famous for their mochi, but also their dramatic mochi-pounding process, a tradition known as mochitsuki in Japanese. We bought two boxes of matcha mochi with red bean filling and peanut powder on the top.

Nakatanidou Mochi Info
Todaiji Temple

Our first stop in Nara was Todaiji Temple or Great Buddha Hall. Constructed in 752, it is one of the most famous temples within Japan and is the world's largest wooden building. It has a 15 meter tall Buddha statue in the main temple room. We admired the outside of the Temple and got our good luck fortunes inside!

Todaiji Temple Info

HIROSHIMA

Hiroshima city is the capital of Hiroshima prefecture in Japan’s Honshu island. It was largely destroyed by an atomic bomb in World War II. We took a bullet train here from Yokohama for a day trip before leaving for the second half of our trip in Osaka. We spent much of the day visiting the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park that memorializes the event in 1945.

Previous Next
Hiroshima Castle

After riding the bullet train, we walked to Hiroshima Castle, an informative museum on Hiroshima and the castle's history with panoramic views of the city. We walked around the castle grounds, much of which are reconstructed and refurbished. We viewed every floor of Hiroshima Castle, which were five floors in total.

Hiroshima Castle Info
Food Highlight: Okonomiyaki

The best meal we had that day was okonomiyaki - a popular fried pancake with batter, cabbage, and a variety of topings ranging from seafood, wasabi, to beef. We got the great experience of being able to practice cooking okonomiyaki ourseves because of the very friendly owner. My parents got a seafood version while my sister and I got the classic.

Okonomiyaki Info
Ginkgo Trees

On our way to the Hiroshima Peace Memorial Park, we saw several Ginkgo trees! They are often seen outside of Shinto shrines or around the city. They are particularly beautiful in Fall because of the bright yellow color that the leaves turn. My family and I loved walking through rows and rows of the ginkgo trees.

Ginkgo Info
Hiroshima Atomic Bomb Dome

We ended the day by visiting the Hiroshima Atomic Bomb Dome and Peace Memorial Park. It commemorates the bombing in World War II and was originally the Prefectural Industrial Promotion Hall. We viewed the dome and then made our way to the rest of the park; it included a museum commermorating victims, a children's memorial, and another monument.

Atomic Bomb Dome Info
